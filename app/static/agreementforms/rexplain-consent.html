Protocol Title: Multimodal Radiology Report to Improve Patient-centered Radiology

Description of Study Population: Adults who have taken a radiology examination within two years.

Key Information

The following is a short summary of this study to help you decide whether or not to participate. More detailed information is listed later on in this form.

Why am I being invited to take part in a research study?

We have invited you to take part in a research study because you have had a radiology examination and received a radiology report about your health condition.

What should I know about a research study?

  • Whether or not you take part is up to you.

  • You can choose not to take part.

  • You can agree to take part and later change your mind.

  • Your decision will not be held against you.

  • You may discuss your decision with your family, your friends and/or your doctor.

  • You can ask all the questions you want before you decide.

Why is this research being done?

This study aims to evaluate the impact of artificial intelligence (AI)-generated video reports on patient understanding of their radiology reports. By watching the video radiology reports, this study aims to determine whether this technology can transfer the information from the original text reports to a patient-centered, easy-to-understand way. The findings from this research will provide valuable information to guide the development and use of AI-assisted patient-centered radiology, possibly leading to improved patient care.

How long will I take part in this research?

Your participation will take approximately 20 minutes, including about 5 minutes to watch your video radiology report and about 15 minutes to take a survey.

What will I be asked to do?

If you decide to participate, you will send us your radiology image (taken within the last two years) and corresponding report. You can send the digital documents to us through email, Google Drive, or any approach you prefer. You will then receive a corresponding video report. You will be asked to watch the video report(s) and complete an online survey for collecting your feedback on the video report(s).

More detailed information about the study procedures can be found under the “What can I expect if I take part in this research?” section.

Is there any way being in this study could be bad for me?

You will be asked to provide the following information: email addresses, age, sex, highest education, your radiology images and reports. We will also collect your feedback on the video reports and will use this information as a part of our analysis, but we will not publish or share the email addresses or any identifiable information with anyone outside of the research group. However, there is always a possibility that this information could be disclosed accidentally. Study staff are trained in privacy and confidentiality, and information will be stored securely, so we believe this risk is minimal.

More detailed information about the risks of this study can be found under the “What are the risks and possible discomforts?” section.

Will being in this study help me in any way?

We cannot promise any benefits to you or others from your taking part in this research. However, possible benefits may include improved understanding of radiology images and contribution to research findings that will inform how AI is used in clinical practice.

What happens if I do not want to be in this research?

Participation in research is voluntary. You can decide to participate, not to participate, or stop participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. Your alternative to participating in this research study is not to participate.

How many people will take part in this research?

About 5-10 people will take part in this research.

What can I expect if I take part in this research?

As a participant, you will be expected to complete the following. Once you have consented to participate, you will send us your radiology image (taken within the past two years) and corresponding report. If you have multiple radiology examinations within two years, you can choose to send one or multiple images and reports. If there is an old radiology examination used for comparison, you can choose to send the old radiology examination image(s) and report(s) to us as well. You can send these to us through email, Google Drive, or any other method you may prefer. For each radiology examination you send to us, you will receive a corresponding video report that was generated using AI. You will be asked to watch the video report(s) and complete an online survey to collect your feedback on the video report(s).

What are my responsibilities?

As a participant, you are responsible for sending us your radiology image(s) and report(s), watching the AI-generated video report(s) we send back to you, and taking an online survey to record your experience with the video reports.

What are the risks and possible discomforts?

You will be asked to provide the following information: email addresses, age, sex, highest education, your radiology images and reports. Your identifiable information will be stored securely on our servers so that we can send you the generated video reports and collect your feedback. Your radiology data will be interpreted by qualified annotators (such as medical students under supervision of board-certified radiologists), but the annotators will only receive de-identified versions of your data with a code to link it back to you. We will securely store this code and will not share it with the annotators. We will not publish or share the email addresses or any identifiable information with anyone outside of the research group. However, there is always a possibility that this information could be disclosed accidentally. Study staff are trained in privacy and confidentiality, and information will be stored securely, so we believe this risk is minimal.

Are there any benefits from being in this research study?

There are no direct benefits to you from your participation in this research. We cannot promise any benefits to you or others from your taking part in this research. However, possible benefits may include improved understanding of radiology images and contributing to research findings that will inform how AI is implemented in clinical practice.

What happens if I say yes, but I change my mind later?

You can leave the research at any time it will not be held against you.

Will I be compensated for participating in this research?

Participants in this study may be compensated $10-$15 or equal-value gift cards for their participation in the study.

What will I have to pay for if I participate in this research?

​​It will not cost you anything to participate in this study aside from your time commitment.

If I take part in this research, how will my privacy be protected? What happens to the information you collect?

Your identifiable information (including email address and contact details) and radiology data will be stored securely on our servers. We will use this information to share video reports and collect your feedback about these reports. Your radiology data will be interpreted by qualified medical personnel, but they will only receive de-identified versions of your data that are coded so that the study team can link it back to you. The code will not be shared with annotators.

Data collected for study purposes will be used for research purposes indefinitely. Your data may be published and shared with other researchers for academic purposes only. However, all of your data will be de-identified before it is shared or included in any publications.

While we will remove personal identifiers before sharing or publishing any data, you should be aware that complete anonymity cannot be guaranteed, particularly with medical imaging data.

A description of this study may be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov. This website will not include information that can identify you. At most, the website will include a summary of the results.

If identifiers are removed from your identifiable private information that is collected during this research, that information could be used for future research studies or distributed to another investigator for future research studies without your additional informed consent.
